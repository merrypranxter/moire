# References

## Artists
- **Hans Jenny** — *Cymatics* (1967). Sound-visualization using sine-wave interference in physical media (powders, fluids). The original "liquid moiré."
- **Carsten Nicolai** — "Moire Index" (2010). Sound and visual interference patterns.
- **Ryoichi Kurokawa** — Audiovisual installations using beat frequencies as primary aesthetic.

## Scientific
- **Fourier Optics** — Sinusoidal gratings are the eigenfunctions of optical systems. All moiré can be decomposed into sine-wave components.
- **Holography** — Interference between reference and object beams creates holograms. Sinusoidal moiré is the 2D analogue.
- **Acoustic Beats** — When two sound waves differ by a few Hz, you hear beats. Sinusoidal moiré is the visual equivalent.
- **Quantum Mechanics** — Wavefunction interference. The mathematics is identical.

## Historical
- **Thomas Young** — Double-slit experiment (1801). The foundational interference demonstration. Sinusoidal moiré is Young's experiment in 2D.
- **Augustin-Jean Fresnel** — Wave theory of light. Fresnel zones are sinusoidal interference patterns.
