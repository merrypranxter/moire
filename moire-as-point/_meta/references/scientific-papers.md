# Scientific Papers & References

## Physics of Moiré

### Twisted Bilayer Graphene
- **Paper:** Cao et al., "Unconventional superconductivity in magic-angle graphene superlattices" (Nature, 2018)
- **Significance:** Discovered that twisting two graphene sheets by ~1.1° creates a moiré superlattice with emergent superconductivity.
- **Moiré connection:** The "magic angle" is where the moiré superlattice becomes flat — the electronic equivalent of visual moiré at specific beat frequencies.

### Supermoiré in Trilayer Graphene
- **Paper:** "Fractal electronic bands from twisted trilayer graphene" (Nature Physics, 2021)
- **Significance:** Three stacked layers create "supermoiré" — fractal energy bands from hierarchical interference.
- **Moiré connection:** The scientific validation of "supermoiré" — moiré of moiré.

### Hofstadter's Butterfly
- **Paper:** Hofstadter, "Energy levels and wave functions of Bloch electrons in rational and irrational magnetic fields" (Physical Review B, 1976)
- **Significance:** Fractal energy spectrum of electrons in magnetic fields.
- **Moiré connection:** The energy-level equivalent of supermoiré — fractal structure at all scales.

## Optical Moiré

### Conoscopic Interferometry
- **Technique:** Uses circular fringe patterns for precision measurement
- **Significance:** Same mathematics as radial moiré, applied to scientific measurement.

### Shadow Moiré Interferometry
- **Technique:** Projects grid onto 3D surface, views through reference grid
- **Significance:** Industrial 3D scanning using moiré. Accuracy to microns.

### Phase-Shifting Profilometry
- **Technique:** Multiple phase-shifted patterns eliminate ambiguity in 3D measurement
- **Significance:** The gold standard for structured light scanning. Used in Kinect, iPhone FaceID.

### Fourier Ptychography
- **Technique:** Computational microscopy using multiple low-res images
- **Significance:** Same frequency-domain logic as moiré magnification.

## Quasicrystals & Quasiperiodicity

### Dan Shechtman's Discovery
- **Paper:** Shechtman et al., "Metallic phase with long-range orientational order and no translational symmetry" (Physical Review Letters, 1984)
- **Significance:** Discovered quasicrystals — materials with Penrose-like atomic structures.
- **Nobel Prize:** 2011 in Chemistry.

### Penrose Tilings
- **Paper:** Penrose, "The role of aesthetics in pure and applied mathematical research" (Bulletin of the Institute of Mathematics and its Applications, 1974)
- **Significance:** Discovered Penrose tilings — quasiperiodic structures with 5-fold symmetry.

### Ammann-Beenker Tilings
- **Significance:** 8-fold quasicrystals. Discovered in metallic alloys and synthetic structures.

## Fourier Analysis

### Fourier Optics
- **Text:** Goodman, "Introduction to Fourier Optics" (1968, multiple editions)
- **Significance:** Sinusoidal gratings are eigenfunctions of optical systems. All moiré can be decomposed into sine-wave components.

### The Sampling Theorem
- **Paper:** Shannon, "Communication in the presence of noise" (Proceedings of the IRE, 1949)
- **Significance:** The Nyquist limit is the condition for avoiding aliasing/moiré.

## Mathematical Foundations

### Voronoi Diagrams
- **Origin:** Georgy Voronoy (1868-1908)
- **Earlier:** Peter Gustav Lejeune Dirichlet (1850) — "Dirichlet tessellations"

### Fibonacci Phyllotaxis
- **Significance:** Plant growth patterns follow Fibonacci/golden angle. Nature's quasiperiodicity.

### Domain Warping
- **Origin:** Inigo Quilez's shader techniques
- **Significance:** Using a function's output to perturb its own input — the mathematical trick behind organic moiré.

## Historical Scientific Context

### Thomas Young's Double-Slit Experiment
- **Year:** 1801
- **Significance:** The foundational interference demonstration. Sinusoidal moiré is Young's experiment in 2D.

### Augustin-Jean Fresnel
- **Work:** Wave theory of light
- **Significance:** Fresnel zones are sinusoidal interference patterns.

### Gustav Hertz
- **Work:** Early experiments with multiple interference gratings (1920s)
- **Significance:** The experimental foundation of supermoiré.
