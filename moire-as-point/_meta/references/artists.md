# Artists Working with Moiré

## Pioneers

### Takahiro Kurashima
- **Work:** *Moiremotion* (2012)
- **Technique:** Spiral and concentric ring moiré in book form. Pages are spiral grids that animate as you flip.
- **Significance:** The definitive moiré art book. Demonstrates that moiré can be a primary medium, not just an effect.

### Gianni Sarcone
- **Work:** Kinegrams and optical illusions (1997-present)
- **Technique:** Barrier grid animation, anamorphic moiré, geometric interference
- **Significance:** The engineer's approach to moiré — precision optical illusion design.

### Rufus Butler Seder
- **Work:** "Scanimation" books — *Gallop!*, *Swing!*, *Waddle!*
- **Technique:** Mass-market kinegram art for children
- **Significance:** Proved moiré can be accessible, commercial, and beloved by millions.

## Op Art & Kinetic Art

### Bridget Riley
- **Work:** "Fall" (1963), "Untitled (La Lune en Rodage)" (1963)
- **Technique:** Geometric paintings that exploit perceptual interference
- **Significance:** The fine art ancestor of digital moiré. Her paintings are "visual beats" in pigment.

### Victor Vasarely
- **Work:** "Vega" series, "Zebra" series
- **Technique:** Geometric abstraction with implicit interference patterns
- **Significance:** The "father" of Op Art. His work anticipates digital moiré by decades.

### Marina Apollonio
- **Work:** "Dinamiche Circolari" series
- **Technique:** Concentric rings with moiré-like perceptual effects
- **Significance:** Italian Op Art's contribution to circular interference.

### Jesús Rafael Soto
- **Work:** "Penetrables" — immersive kinetic installations
- **Technique:** Layered linear elements that create moiré-like interference when viewer moves
- **Significance:** Physical moiré that requires bodily movement to experience.

## Contemporary Digital Artists

### Inigo Quilez (iq)
- **Work:** ShaderToy contributions, "Happy Jumping"
- **Technique:** Domain warping, perturbed grids, organic interference
- **Significance:** The shader artist's shader artist. His techniques are the foundation of modern moiré coding.

### Patricio Gonzalez Vivo
- **Work:** "The Book of Shaders"
- **Technique:** Educational shader art, noise and domain warping
- **Significance:** Democratized shader knowledge — the textbook for moiré artists.

### Tyler Hobbs
- **Work:** "Fidenza" (Art Blocks), generative line art
- **Technique:** Flow fields, perturbation, organic interference
- **Significance:** Proved moiré-adjacent techniques can be commercially successful in NFT/generative art.

### Manolo Gamboa Naon
- **Work:** "Variations" series
- **Technique:** Perturbed grids, interference patterns, Argentine generative art
- **Significance:** South American perspective on computational interference.

### Andy Lomas
- **Work:** "Aggregation" series
- **Technique:** Cellular growth simulations, Voronoi-like structures
- **Significance:** The organic computational aesthetic — moiré from simulated biology.

### Casey Reas
- **Work:** "Process" series, "{Software} Structures"
- **Technique:** Software systems that generate emergent patterns
- **Significance:** The process art approach to moiré — the system is the artist.

### Roman Verostko
- **Work:** Algorithmic drawings with recursive structures
- **Technique:** Pen plotter art with systematic variation
- **Significance:** The grandfather of algorithmic art. His recursive structures are the ancestor of fractal moiré.

### Manfred Mohr
- **Work:** "P-197" and other algorithmic drawings
- **Technique:** Systematic exploration of combinatorial pattern generation
- **Significance:** The rigorous, systematic approach to generative geometry.

### Vera Molnár
- **Work:** "Des Ordres" (1974), "Lettres de ma mère"
- **Technique:** Systematic variation of geometric parameters
- **Significance:** The method behind supermoiré — systematic exploration of parameter space.

## Light & Installation Artists

### Olafur Eliasson
- **Work:** "The Weather Project" (2003), "Your Colour Memory" (2004)
- **Technique:** Light, shadow, color as primary media
- **Significance:** Not moiré per se, but the same obsession with light as phenomenon.

### Anthony McCall
- **Work:** "Line Describing a Cone" (1973)
- **Technique:** Projected light as solid form
- **Significance:** The projection-as-object principle — light interference as sculpture.

### James Turrell
- **Work:** Roden Crater, Skyspaces
- **Technique:** Light and color as primary media
- **Significance:** The phenomenological approach to visual perception.

### Carsten Nicolai
- **Work:** "Moire Index" (2010), "Grid Index"
- **Technique:** Sound and visual beat frequencies
- **Significance:** The audio-visual moiré specialist.

### Ryoichi Kurokawa
- **Work:** Audiovisual installations
- **Technique:** Tight coupling of sound and visual interference
- **Significance:** The synesthetic moiré experience.

### Ryoji Ikeda
- **Work:** "datamatics", "test pattern"
- **Technique:** Pure data visualization with audio-reactive aesthetics
- **Significance:** The data moiré artist — information made visible through systematic interference.

## Anamorphic & Architectural

### Felice Varini
- **Work:** "Three Ellipses for Three Locks" (2000), architectural anamorphosis
- **Technique:** Geometric shapes that only resolve from one viewpoint
- **Significance:** The master of observer-dependent art — the physical analogue of anamorphic moiré.

### Patrick Hughes
- **Work:** "Reverspective" paintings
- **Technique:** Rooms that appear to move as you walk past — inverted perspective
- **Significance:** The perspective-as-motion principle.

### István Orosz
- **Work:** Anamorphic portraits hidden in chaotic images
- **Technique:** Hidden images that resolve from specific viewpoints
- **Significance:** The moiré-as-secret principle in traditional media.

### Jonty Hurwitz
- **Work:** Anamorphic sculptures
- **Technique:** Distorted metal forms that resolve into recognizable shapes
- **Significance:** The 3D anamorphic moiré — physical interference of form.

## Data Visualization

### Giorgia Lupi & Stefanie Posavec
- **Work:** "Dear Data" — hand-drawn data visualization
- **Technique:** Personal data as visual art
- **Significance:** The analog ancestor of data moiré — information made beautiful through systematic representation.

### Moritz Stefaner
- **Work:** "Truth & Beauty Operations"
- **Technique:** Data visualization as aesthetic practice
- **Significance:** The data-as-art approach that data moiré extends.

### Nicholas Felton
- **Work:** "Annual Reports" — personal data visualization
- **Significance:** The data-as-self principle — quantified life as visual art.
