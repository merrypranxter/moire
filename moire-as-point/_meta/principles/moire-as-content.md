# Principle: Moiré as Content, Not Artifact

## The Core Shift

In traditional design and engineering, moiré is a **defect** to be eliminated:
- Printers fight moiré in halftone screens
- Photographers avoid moiré with anti-aliasing filters
- Display engineers suppress moiré with subpixel rendering

In moiré-as-point art, moiré is the **subject** to be amplified:
- The grids are the "code"
- The moiré is the "runtime"
- The interference is the "visible entity"

## The Ontological Flip

| Traditional View | Moiré-as-Point View |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| Grids are real; moiré is illusion | Moiré is real; grids are infrastructure |
| Clean signal is goal | Signal + interference = the art |
| Aliasing is error | Aliasing is feature |
| Periodicity is desired | Aperiodicity is desired |
| Subpixel precision hides artifacts | Subpixel precision reveals emergent patterns |

## Practical Implications

1. **Don't anti-alias the grids** — let them alias. The aliasing creates the moiré.
2. **Don't match the scales** — the difference *is* the pattern.
3. **Don't stabilize the motion** — the drift *is* the animation.
4. **Don't suppress the beats** — the beats *are* the music.

## The Compusophy Reading

> "The visible world is the beat between invisible worlds."

The grids are ontology (what exists). The moiré is phenomenology (what appears). The artist's job is not to create the visible, but to engineer the conditions under which the visible emerges.
