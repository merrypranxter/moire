# Principle 10: Moiré is Compression Leakage

Digital moiré reveals **the mismatch between continuous reality and discrete representation**. It is the residue of sampling and quantization — what leaks out when a continuous signal is forced through a finite grid of pixels, samples, or bits.

Every digital moiré pattern is, in this sense, an honest report from the encoder: *here is where my grid couldn't keep up with the world*.
