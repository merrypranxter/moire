# References

## Artists
- **István Orosz** — Anamorphic art with hidden images. The moiré magnification principle in traditional media.
- **Lenticular Artists** — Lenticular printing uses similar principles: different images visible from different angles.
- **Holographic Artists** — Holograms are interference patterns that reconstruct images. Moiré magnification is a 2D analogue.

## Scientific
- **Moiré Magnification** — Optical technique for measuring deformation. A grid printed on a surface is viewed through a reference grid; the moiré reveals strain.
- **Superresolution Microscopy** — Techniques like STED and PALM use interference principles to beat the diffraction limit. The scientific equivalent of moiré magnification.
- **Fourier Ptychography** — Computational microscopy using multiple low-res images to reconstruct high-res. Same frequency-domain logic as moiré magnification.

## Historical
- **Camera Obscura** — The original "pinhole lens." Light through a small hole projects an inverted image.
- **Pinhole Photography** — Lensless photography. The pinhole acts as a crude lens — exactly the physical analogue of moiré magnification.
- **Pepper's Ghost** — 19th century illusion. Reflection-based image projection — the theatrical ancestor of moiré magnification.
