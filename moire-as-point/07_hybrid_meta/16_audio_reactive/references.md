# References

## Artists
- **Ryoichi Kurokawa** — Audiovisual installations. Tight coupling of sound and visual interference.
- **Carsten Nicolai** — "Moire Index" (2010). Sound and visual beat frequencies as primary material.
- **Ryoji Ikeda** — "datamatics" and "test pattern." Pure data visualization with audio-reactive aesthetics.
- **Max Cooper** — Music videos with generative visuals. "Emergence" series maps musical structure to visual emergence.

## Scientific
- **Fourier Transform** — The mathematical bridge between time and frequency. Audio FFT is identical to spatial Fourier analysis of moiré.
- **Chladni Figures** — 18th century. Sound frequencies create visual patterns in sand on vibrating plates. The physical ancestor of audio-reactive moiré.
- **Cymatics** — Hans Jenny (1967). Sound visualization in physical media. The organic equivalent of shader-based audio reactivity.
- **Sonification** — The inverse: mapping visual data to sound. Audio-reactive moiré is "visualization" — the same mathematics in reverse.

## Historical
- **Oscilloscope Art** — 1950s-60s. Lissajous figures and other patterns from audio signals on CRT displays.
- **Laser Shows** — 1970s onward. Audio-driven laser interference patterns at concerts.
- **Music Visualization Software** — Winamp, iTunes visualizers. The consumer precursor to shader-based audio reactivity.
