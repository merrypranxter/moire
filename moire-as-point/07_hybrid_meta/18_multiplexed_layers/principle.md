# 18 Multiplexed Layers

This family explores interference patterns generated by stacking three or more grids with different angular offsets, frequencies and opacities. When multiple lattice layers interact, the resulting moiré becomes dense, cathedral‑like and deeply hierarchical. Subfolders could include triadic interference (three lattices), nested frequency stacks (harmonic ratios), octave lattices, and phase‑lag braids.
