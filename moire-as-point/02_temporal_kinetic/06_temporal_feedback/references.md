# References

## Artists
- **Nam June Paik** — "TV Buddha" (1974). Video feedback as meditation. The feedback loop as subject.
- **Bill Viola** — "The Reflecting Pool" (1977). Slow-motion temporal manipulation. Memory made visible.
- **Steina and Woody Vasulka** — Video synthesizer pioneers. Feedback as generative method.
- **Rosa Menkman** — "The Glitch Moment(um)" (2011). Feedback artifacts as aesthetic.

## Scientific
- **Video Feedback Physics** — The mathematics of video feedback (pointing a camera at its own monitor) is identical to laser cavity dynamics and cellular automata.
- **Iconic Memory** — George Sperling (1960). Visual persistence lasts ~250ms. Feedback decay mimics this.
- **Echoic Memory** — Auditory equivalent. The delay between sound and reflection creates temporal beats.
- **Reverberation** — In acoustics, feedback (echo) creates standing waves. Same mathematics, different medium.

## Historical
- **Feedback Music** — Terry Riley, Steve Reich. Phasing patterns where one tape loop drifts against another. The audio equivalent of temporal moiré.
- **Delay Pedals** — Electric guitar effects. Analog bucket-brigade delays create temporal texture.
- **Video Art History** — 1960s-70s video feedback loops were among the first real-time generative art systems.
