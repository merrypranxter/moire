# References

## Artists & Practitioners
- **Rufus Butler Seder** — "Scanimation" books: *Gallop!*, *Swing!*, *Waddle!*. Mass-market kinegram art. The most accessible entry point.
- **Gianni Sarcone** — Kinegram pioneer since 1997. "Sarcone's Kinegrams" — optical illusion engineering at its finest.
- **Lenticular printing industry** — Hundreds of commercial applications: postcards, packaging, advertising. The industrial-scale moiré.

## Scientific
- **Barrier Grid Animation** — Invented in the 1890s. One of the earliest motion picture technologies before film.
- **Parallax Barrier Displays** — Nintendo 3DS, glasses-free 3D. Uses the same principle for depth, not time.
- **Autostereoscopy** — Lenticular and barrier methods for 3D without glasses. Moiré as depth carrier.

## Historical
- **Émile Reynaud** — "Praxinoscope" (1877). Early animation device using mirrors and strips. Predecessor to kinegram logic.
- **Eadweard Muybridge** — Motion photography (1878). The content that kinegrams would later animate.
- **William Friese-Greene** — "Biocolour" process. Early color film using similar interlacing principles.
