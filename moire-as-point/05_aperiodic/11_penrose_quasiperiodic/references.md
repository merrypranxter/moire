# References

## Artists
- **Ammann, Robert** — Discovered the Ammann bars in Penrose tilings. The "hidden order" that moiré reveals.
- **Penrose, Roger** — Discovered Penrose tilings (1974). Nobel Prize in Physics (2020) for black hole work, but the tilings are his most visual contribution.
- **M.C. Escher** — "Circle Limit" series. Hyperbolic tilings with similar "impossible" symmetry properties.

## Scientific
- **Quasicrystals** — Dan Shechtman discovered quasicrystals (1982), winning Nobel Prize (2011). Aluminum-manganese alloys with Penrose-like atomic structures.
- **Fibonacci Phyllotaxis** — Plant growth patterns follow Fibonacci/golden angle. Nature's quasiperiodicity.
- **Ammann-Beenker Tilings** — 8-fold quasicrystals. Discovered in metallic alloys and synthetic structures.
- **Photonic Quasicrystals** — Materials with quasiperiodic refractive index. Control light in ways periodic crystals cannot.

## Historical
- **Kepler** — "Harmonices Mundi" (1619). Early exploration of pentagonal tilings — the origin of the idea.
- **Islamic Geometric Art** — Girih tiles in medieval Islamic architecture. Some patterns are quasiperiodic — 500 years before Penrose.
- **Albrecht Dürer** — "Melencolia I" (1514). The magic square and geometric order — early European fascination with mathematical patterns.
