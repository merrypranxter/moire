# References

## Artists
- **Felice Varini** — The master of architectural anamorphosis. "Three Ellipses for Three Locks" (2000). Geometric shapes that only resolve from one viewpoint.
- **Patrick Hughes** — "Reverspective" paintings. Rooms that appear to move as you walk past — the perspective is inverted.
- **István Orosz** — Anamorphic portraits hidden in chaotic images. The moiré-as-secret principle in traditional media.
- **Jonty Hurwitz** — Anamorphic sculptures. Distorted metal forms that resolve into recognizable shapes only from one angle.

## Scientific
- **Anamorphic Projection** — Projective geometry. The mathematics of perspective distortion and its inverse.
- **Parallax Barriers** — Autostereoscopic displays. The same principle as anamorphic moiré but for 3D rather than pattern resolution.
- **Pepper's Ghost** — 19th century stage illusion. Reflection-based anamorphosis.

## Historical
- **Hans Holbein the Younger** — "The Ambassadors" (1533). The skull anamorphosis in the foreground — the original anamorphic secret.
- **Leonardo da Vinci** — Early anamorphic experiments in notebooks.
- **Baroque Ceiling Painting** — Trompe-l'œil that only works from the church entrance. The architectural anamorphosis tradition.
