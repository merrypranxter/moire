# Variations

## 1. Multi-Secret Viewpoints
Design multiple "sweet spots" — each reveals a different moiré pattern. Walk around the installation and different secrets appear at different positions. Like a gallery where each spot is a different painting.

## 2. Temporal Sweet Spot
The "correct" viewpoint changes over time. The viewer must *track* the secret — it moves, and you follow. Creates a chase dynamic.

## 3. Stereo Anamorphosis
Left and right eyes see different moiré patterns. The brain fuses them into a 3D secret that only exists in binocular vision. Close one eye, and it vanishes.

## 4. Mirror-Dependent
The moiré only resolves when viewed through a specific curved mirror. The mirror's distortion compensates for the grid distortion. Remove the mirror, see noise.

## 5. Cylindrical Wrap
Grids are printed on a cylinder. The moiré only resolves when you stand at the cylinder's center — the radial symmetry creates a 360° sweet spot, but only at the correct radius.

## 6. Parallax Barrier Secret
Use a parallax barrier (like Nintendo 3DS) so each eye sees a different grid. The moiré is *stereoscopic* — it has depth that only resolves with both eyes.
