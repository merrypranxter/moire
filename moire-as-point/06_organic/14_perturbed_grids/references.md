# References

## Artists
- **Inigo Quilez** — "Happy Jumping" and other shader art. Extensive use of domain warping and perturbation for organic effects.
- **Patricio Gonzalez Vivo** — "The Book of Shaders." Chapter on noise and domain warping.
- **Tyler Hobbs** — "Fidenza" and other generative art. Flow fields and perturbation for organic line art.
- **Manolo Gamboa Naon** — "Variations" series. Perturbed grids and interference in Argentine generative art.

## Scientific
- **Turbulence Theory** — Kolmogorov's energy cascade. Multi-scale perturbation is the physics of turbulence.
- **Reaction-Diffusion** — Alan Turing (1952). "The Chemical Basis of Morphogenesis." Pattern formation from perturbed uniformity.
- **Domain Warping** — Inigo Quilez's technique. Using a function's output to perturb its own input.
- **Flow Fields** — Vector fields that guide particle/grid motion. The mathematics of fluid dynamics applied to visual art.

## Historical
- **Jackson Pollock** — "Drip" paintings. The physical perturbation of paint by gravity and motion — organic interference in analog media.
- **Cézanne** — "Passage" technique. Brushstrokes that dissolve boundaries — the painterly equivalent of perturbed grids.
- **Japanese Sumi-e** — Ink wash painting. The unpredictable flow of ink on paper — natural perturbation as aesthetic.
